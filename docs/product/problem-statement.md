# Problem Statement

## Summary

Recruiters often have useful candidate data in spreadsheets, ATS exports, CV folders, Notion databases, Google Sheets, and other semi-structured sources, but they rarely reuse that Talent Pool effectively when a new search starts.

The core problem is not lack of candidate data. It is lack of fast access, memory, and reuse.

## Target User

The first target user is a recruiter who already maintains candidate data but does not have a reliable way to query it quickly.

Best-fit early segments:

- Freelance recruiters managing candidate spreadsheets manually.
- Small recruiting agencies with inconsistent ATS usage.
- Talent acquisition consultants who reuse old searches and referrals.
- Small HR teams with candidate exports but no strong search workflow.

The strongest early user is likely someone who says a version of:

> I know I have candidates somewhere, but finding the right ones again takes too long, so I often start from scratch.

## Current Workarounds

Recruiters currently solve this problem by:

- Searching spreadsheets manually with filters and keyword search.
- Looking through old ATS records or exported CSV files.
- Searching email, WhatsApp, LinkedIn conversations, and CV folders.
- Asking colleagues if they remember someone relevant.
- Re-sourcing externally on LinkedIn instead of reusing internal data.
- Maintaining ad hoc notes in Notion, Airtable, Google Sheets, or local files.

These workflows are fragmented, slow, and depend heavily on recruiter memory.

## Pain

The pain is strongest when:

- A new search resembles previous searches.
- The recruiter has a large enough Talent Pool that memory is unreliable.
- Candidate Records are incomplete, stale, duplicated, or inconsistently formatted.
- The recruiter needs a Shortlist quickly.
- The recruiter must explain why a candidate is relevant to a client or hiring manager.

The pain is weaker when:

- The recruiter works with very small candidate volumes.
- The recruiter already has excellent ATS discipline.
- The recruiter mostly sources externally and rarely reuses old candidate data.
- Data privacy concerns block any upload workflow.

## Product Thesis

Talent Rediscovery should make an existing Talent Pool feel like active recruiting memory.

Instead of manually filtering rigid fields, the recruiter submits a Search Request in natural language and receives a Shortlist from their own data with reasons, evidence, gaps, and Suggested Next Actions.

## Validation Verdict

**Needs more validation.**

The problem is plausible and well-defined, but the project should not treat it as validated until recruiters confirm that:

- They already have underused candidate data.
- Searching that data is slow or unreliable today.
- They would trust a Shortlist if it included evidence and gaps.
- They would upload or share a Talent Pool file in some acceptable form.
- They would pay for either a manual rediscovery service or a productized workflow.

## Red Flags To Test

- Recruiters may not have enough structured historical candidate data.
- Their ATS may already solve this well enough for the best-funded segment.
- Sensitive candidate data may make uploads hard without strong privacy guarantees.
- Recruiters may value sourcing new candidates more than rediscovering existing ones.
- AI-assisted rankings may be distrusted unless every Match is grounded in transparent Candidate Record evidence.
- Voice Copilot may be demo-friendly and useful as an interaction layer, but text must remain the reliable core workflow.

## Green Flags To Look For

- Recruiters already maintain spreadsheets or exports because existing tools are insufficient.
- They can name recent searches where old candidates would have helped.
- They have manually searched old files and found useful candidates before.
- They are willing to send an anonymized sample file for a manual test.
- They ask for the tool before it exists.
- They would pay for a one-off Talent Pool audit, Shortlist generation, or recurring search assistant.
