# Community Research

## Goal

Identify reachable recruiter communities where Talent Rediscovery can validate the pain before building a full product.

The immediate goal is 10 problem interviews and at least 1 concierge test with a real or anonymized Talent Pool file.

## Best Initial Communities

### Freelance Recruiters

Persistent problem:

- They often manage candidates in spreadsheets, notes, old searches, LinkedIn messages, and lightweight trackers.
- They have direct financial incentives to move faster on each search.
- They may lack mature ATS workflows.

Why this community is attractive:

- Short decision cycles.
- Easier access to the person who feels the pain and can pay.
- More likely to accept manual or concierge help.

Where to reach them:

- LinkedIn search for freelance recruiter, independent recruiter, talent acquisition consultant, sourcer.
- Recruiting and sourcing Slack or Discord groups.
- Local HR and recruiting communities.
- Referrals from founders, startup operators, and agency owners.

Validation angle:

- Ask how they reuse old candidates when a new client search arrives.
- Offer a manual rediscovery report for one active or recent role.

### Small Recruiting Agencies

Persistent problem:

- Agencies accumulate candidate data across multiple recruiters, clients, roles, and tools.
- Candidate rediscovery can be blocked by inconsistent data entry and weak internal memory.
- A candidate found by one recruiter may be invisible to another.

Why this community is attractive:

- The value of rediscovery compounds across multiple recruiters.
- Agencies can pay if a workflow improves fill speed or candidate reuse.
- There is a clear operational pain around fragmented Talent Pools.

Where to reach them:

- LinkedIn lists of boutique recruiting agencies.
- Agency founders and directors of recruitment.
- Recruiting events and webinars.
- Local business and HR associations.

Validation angle:

- Ask whether recruiters search internal Talent Pools before sourcing externally.
- Ask what percentage of placements come from existing Talent Pool reuse.
- Offer a Talent Pool audit or one-role Shortlist from exported data.

### Talent Acquisition Consultants

Persistent problem:

- Consultants often work across clients and projects, building fragmented candidate memory over time.
- They may have valuable candidate notes that are hard to query later.

Why this community is attractive:

- They understand the value of process improvement.
- They may influence agency or client workflows.
- They can explain the buying process and privacy concerns.

Where to reach them:

- LinkedIn posts and comments around sourcing, recruitment operations, talent intelligence, and ATS workflows.
- Consultant newsletters or communities.
- Personal network introductions.

Validation angle:

- Ask what they do when historical candidate notes are spread across tools.
- Ask whether explained Shortlist generation would improve client delivery.

## Secondary Communities

### Internal HR Teams At Small Companies

Potential value:

- They may have Candidate Records from previous openings.
- They may want to reuse applicants before paying for new sourcing.

Risks:

- Hiring may be less frequent.
- Data privacy and procurement may slow validation.
- They may depend more on an ATS than spreadsheets.

Use this segment for learning, not as the first wedge unless interviews show stronger pain.

### Sourcers

Potential value:

- Sourcers understand search workflows deeply.
- They can stress-test Match quality and evidence requirements.

Risks:

- Some sourcers focus more on external sourcing than internal rediscovery.
- They may see the tool as adjacent rather than core.

Use this segment to sharpen ranking and evidence requirements.

## Discovery Signals To Track

For every conversation, record:

- Segment.
- Current candidate data location.
- Current search workflow.
- Last time they reused an old candidate.
- Last time they failed to find an old candidate.
- Estimated time spent searching internal data.
- Willingness to share anonymized data.
- Trust requirements for recommendations.
- Pricing reaction.
- Next action agreed.

## Recommended First 20 Outreach Targets

Start with people who can say yes without procurement:

- 8 freelance recruiters.
- 6 boutique agency founders or recruiting managers.
- 4 talent acquisition consultants.
- 2 sourcers who can critique matching quality.

## Outreach Priorities

Prioritize targets who mention at least one of these in their profile or content:

- Recruiting for startups or tech roles.
- Freelance or independent recruiting.
- Candidate pipeline management.
- Talent sourcing.
- Recruiting operations.
- ATS implementation or dissatisfaction.
- Spreadsheet-heavy workflows.

## Community Verdict

The first wedge should be freelance recruiters and small recruiting agencies.

They are reachable, likely to have messy candidate data, and can validate the core pain without long procurement cycles.
